COMMON CROP DISEASES AND PESTS IN INDIA — ORGANIC REMEDIES

== HOW TO DESCRIBE SYMPTOMS ==
When a farmer describes a problem, look for:
- Which part is affected: leaves, stem, fruit, root
- Color change: yellow, brown, black, white powder
- Texture: spots, holes, wilting, rotting, curling
- Pattern: starts from bottom leaves or top; spreads fast or slow
- Insects present: yes/no, color, size
- Smell: rotten, sour, musty

== FUNGAL DISEASES ==

1. Powdery Mildew (Safed Churn Rog / Safedi)
Symptoms: White powdery coating on leaves and stems; looks like someone sprinkled white flour
Crops: Wheat, cucumber, pumpkin, grapes, okra, peas
Season: Cool nights + warm days (October-February in North India)
Likely Cause: Fungal infection, thrives in humid conditions with poor airflow
Organic Remedy:
  - Mix 1 teaspoon baking soda (khane ka soda) + 1 liter water + 5-6 drops neem oil; spray on affected plants
  - Spray diluted cow urine (1 part urine : 10 parts water) every week
  - Apply Jeevamrutha as foliar spray early morning
  - Remove severely infected leaves and burn them (do not compost)
Prevention: Plant with proper spacing; avoid overhead watering; spray preventive neem oil

2. Late Blight / Jhulsa Rog
Symptoms: Dark brown water-soaked blotches on leaves; white fluffy mold on leaf undersides; spreads rapidly; black lesions on stems; rotting of fruit
Crops: Potato, tomato
Season: Monsoon and post-monsoon (July-October)
Likely Cause: Phytophthora infestans; cool + wet weather combination
Organic Remedy:
  - Remove and BURN all affected plant parts immediately
  - Spray Bordeaux mixture (1% copper sulfate + 1% lime in water) — traditional and effective
  - Spray fermented cow urine (1:20 dilution) every 5 days
  - Apply Trichoderma viride (2g per liter water) soil drench
Prevention: Avoid overhead irrigation; ensure good drainage; do not plant tomato/potato in same field consecutively

3. Damping Off (Nursery Rot)
Symptoms: Seedlings collapse and fall over; stem looks pinched/rotten at soil surface; healthy above, rotten at base
Crops: Tomato, chilli, brinjal, cabbage seedlings in nursery
Likely Cause: Pythium, Rhizoctonia fungi; caused by overwatering and poor drainage
Organic Remedy:
  - Drench nursery with Trichoderma viride solution (5g per liter water)
  - Apply Beejamrutha seed treatment before sowing (prevents this disease)
  - Sprinkle wood ash or sand on nursery bed surface
  - Remove and discard affected seedlings immediately
Prevention: Do not overwater; use well-drained potting mix; sow at correct depth; provide good sunlight

4. Leaf Spot (Patti Ke Dabbe)
Symptoms: Brown, black, or grey circular spots on leaves; sometimes with yellow border around the spot
Crops: Rice, groundnut, sorghum, cotton, vegetables
Likely Cause: Various fungi (Alternaria, Cercospora, Helminthosporium)
Organic Remedy:
  - Spray neem oil (5ml per liter water) every 7-10 days
  - Garlic spray: crush 10 garlic cloves in 1 liter water, strain, spray
  - Remove infected leaves; do not compost them
Prevention: Avoid splashing irrigation water on leaves; proper plant spacing for airflow

5. Rust (Ratua / Geruwa)
Symptoms: Orange, reddish-brown powdery pustules on lower leaf surface; yellow spots visible on upper surface
Crops: Wheat, barley, sugarcane, beans, groundnut
Organic Remedy:
  - Spray cow urine (diluted 1:5 with water) — traditional remedy with antifungal properties
  - Neem oil spray (10ml per liter) every 5-7 days
  - Bordeaux mixture as protective spray before infection
Prevention: Plant resistant varieties; balanced soil nutrition (excess nitrogen increases rust susceptibility)

6. Wilt (Murjhaan)
Two types:
A) Fusarium Wilt: Plants yellow from bottom up, then wilt; brown/reddish vascular discoloration inside stem
Crops: Tomato, brinjal, chilli, cotton, banana
B) Bacterial Wilt: Sudden collapse of entire plant; white sticky ooze visible when stem cut
Crops: Tomato, potato, brinjal, groundnut

Organic Remedy (Fusarium):
  - Soil drench with Trichoderma viride (5g/liter) or Pseudomonas fluorescens (2g/liter)
  - Add well-composted FYM and neem cake to soil before planting
  - Solarize soil in summer (cover with clear plastic sheet for 4-6 weeks before planting)
Organic Remedy (Bacterial):
  - Remove and burn infected plants immediately
  - Lime (chuna) application to soil raises pH, reduces bacteria
  - Long crop rotation — avoid same family for 3-4 years
Prevention: Crop rotation is most important; use healthy disease-free seedlings; improve drainage

== PEST PROBLEMS ==

7. Aphids (Mahu / Chipkali Keede)
Symptoms: Tiny soft insects (green, black, brown) clustered on new growth, buds, flower stems; sticky honeydew on leaves; leaves curl
Crops: Almost all vegetables, mustard, chilli, citrus
Organic Remedy:
  - Neem oil (5ml) + liquid soap/dish wash (2ml) per liter water; spray every 5-7 days
  - Strong jet of water to knock off aphids (early morning)
  - Plant marigold (genda) nearby — repels aphids
  - Release or attract ladybird beetles (chitroli) — they eat aphids
  - Spray diluted cow urine (1:10)
Prevention: Companion plant with marigold and coriander; do not over-apply nitrogen (lush growth attracts aphids)

8. Whitefly (Safed Makkhi)
Symptoms: Tiny white flies (2mm) on leaf undersides; fly up in cloud when plant disturbed; yellowing leaves; sticky residue
Crops: Tomato, cotton, okra, brinjal, cucumber
Organic Remedy:
  - Yellow sticky traps (yellow cardboard coated with grease/castor oil) — very effective
  - Neem oil spray (10ml per liter) weekly
  - Garlic + chilli extract spray (boil 50g each in 1 liter water, cool, strain, spray)
Prevention: Reflective silver mulch on soil confuses whiteflies; remove weeds; avoid excess nitrogen

9. Stem Borer (Tana Bhedhak)
Symptoms: Dead heart (central shoot dies while outer leaves remain green); larvae visible inside stem when cut; exit holes with frass
Crops: Rice, sugarcane, maize, sorghum, bajra
Organic Remedy:
  - Remove dead hearts immediately (pull out infested tiller)
  - Spray neem seed kernel extract (NSKE) 5% — crush neem seeds, soak overnight, strain
  - Trichogramma parasitic wasp release — biological control
  - Light traps at night to catch adult moths
Prevention: Early planting; balanced nutrition; do not leave crop residues in field after harvest

10. Fruit Borer / Pod Borer (Fal Bhedhak)
Symptoms: Round holes in fruits/pods; larvae inside eating seeds; frass near entry hole; fruit/pod rots from inside
Crops: Tomato, brinjal, chilli, okra, chickpea, pigeonpea
Organic Remedy:
  - Spray Bt (Bacillus thuringiensis) — a natural bacteria that kills caterpillars only
  - Neem oil spray at pea-sized fruit stage
  - Remove and destroy infested fruits (do not leave on plant or ground)
  - Pheromone traps to monitor and reduce moth population
Prevention: Early sowing; plant marigold borders; encourage natural predators

11. Root Knot Nematode (Jad Ganth)
Symptoms: Stunted growth; yellowing despite watering; swellings/galls visible on roots when pulled out
Crops: Vegetables (tomato, brinjal, okra), groundnut
Organic Remedy:
  - Add generous amounts of well-composted FYM to soil before planting
  - Apply neem cake (250kg per acre) to soil
  - Plant marigold (especially Tagetes patula — French marigold) as intercrop or prior crop
  - Drench with Pseudomonas fluorescens solution
Prevention: Crop rotation; grow marigold green manure crop and incorporate into soil; avoid continuous vegetable cultivation

12. Mites (Red Spider Mite / Laal Keeda)
Symptoms: Fine web on leaf undersides; tiny red/yellow dots moving; leaves turn yellow-bronze, dry out; worst in dry hot weather
Crops: Brinjal, tomato, okra, cotton
Organic Remedy:
  - Spray water forcefully (especially leaf undersides)
  - Neem oil spray (10ml per liter) every 5 days
  - Garlic extract spray
  - Increase humidity around plants
Prevention: Maintain soil moisture; avoid water stress; intercrop with aromatic herbs

== SOIL AND NUTRITIONAL PROBLEMS ==

13. Soil Hardness / Crusting (Mitti Ka Kathora Hona)
Symptoms: Water pools on surface and doesn't penetrate; soil surface cracks; stunted growth despite watering
Cause: Compaction from heavy machinery, excess irrigation, no organic matter
Remedy:
  - Apply Jeevamrutha regularly — soil microbes break up compaction naturally
  - Add compost/FYM generously (2-5 tons per acre)
  - Grow deep-rooted cover crops (pigeon pea, sunflower) to break up soil
  - Minimum tillage — do not plough too deep or too frequently

14. Yellowing Leaves (Patti Peeli Hona)
Many possible causes — identify by pattern:
- Lower leaves yellow first = Nitrogen deficiency → Apply Jeevamrutha, green manure
- Young top leaves yellow, veins remain green = Iron or Zinc deficiency → Spray fermented cow urine + neem
- All leaves uniformly pale = waterlogging or root damage → Improve drainage
- Yellow with small insects = aphids or mites → Treat pests first
- Yellow patches on leaves = fungal disease → Identify specific disease, apply neem oil

== PANCHAGAVYA (FIVE COW PRODUCTS TONIC) ==

Panchagavya boosts plant immunity and growth. Like a health tonic for plants.

Recipe:
- 3 kg fresh cow dung
- 2 liters cow urine
- 2 liters cow milk
- 2 liters cow curd (dahi)
- 500g cow ghee
- 500g banana (ripe, mashed)
- 500g coconut water or tender coconut pulp
- 1 kg jaggery

Preparation: Mix cow dung + ghee first, ferment 3 days. Then add other ingredients. Ferment for 30 days total, stirring daily. Final mixture: dark liquid with fermented smell.

Use: Dilute 3% (30ml per liter water) and spray on crops. Apply every 15-21 days.

Benefits: Increases yield 10-15%; improves plant immunity; enhances fruit quality; promotes root growth

== NEEM-BASED PEST MANAGEMENT ==

Neem (Azadirachta indica) is the most powerful organic pest manager available in India:

1. Neem Oil Spray: 5-10ml neem oil + 1-2ml liquid soap per liter water
   - Works against: aphids, whitefly, scale insects, mites, young caterpillars
   - How: Disrupts insect life cycle, acts as antifeedant, repellent
   - Apply in early morning or evening; not in hot midday sun

2. Neem Seed Kernel Extract (NSKE): 50g neem seeds soaked overnight in 1 liter water, strained
   - Highly effective against stem borer and fruit borer caterpillars
   - 5% solution (50g per liter) is standard

3. Neem Cake: Dried neem seed residue after oil extraction
   - Soil application at 250-500 kg per acre
   - Controls soil nematodes, white grubs, termites
   - Also adds nutrition to soil

4. Neem Leaf Tea: Boil 1 kg neem leaves in 10 liters water for 20 minutes; cool, strain
   - Spray on plants for general pest repellence
